Hotel rates run 40–57% above normal, and the Nozomi shinkansen drops its non-reserved cars entirely — no walk-up seats. The crowds are domestic, not foreign: Japanese families, going where you'd go. Both itineraries below solve this the same way, which is the way the Israeli Facebook groups solve it: don't move between cities during it. One exception, and it's deliberate. You leave Tokyo on Wed 23 Sep — the Equinox, the last day of the run, and the heaviest return-travel day of the five. It's payable: reserve your seat the minute the 30-day booking window opens, travel mid-morning rather than in the evening surge, and remember that your leg is outbound from Tokyo while most of the traffic is heading back into it.

The shape of the trip

Part-1 things that will otherwise catch you out

Dashi hides in "vegetarian" dishes

Bonito/fish stock is in miso soup, many "veg" broths, tamago and dipping sauces. For Noa, lean on the clearly-safe categories — yakitori, chicken ramen/paitan, curry, chicken-katsu — and ask before the otoshi cover lands at an izakaya.

Official ticket sites reject foreign Visa

teamLab and Shibuya Sky official pages have bounced foreign-issued Visa cards since 2024. Use Klook (separate international allocation, often available when official shows sold out) or pay with Mastercard/Amex.

Sunset slots are a midnight sport

Shibuya Sky sunset releases 14 days out at 00:00 JST and goes in minutes. If you miss it, book a slot 30–40 min before sunset that you can get — once you're up, you stay as long as you like — or take a full-night slot for the neon view.

Takeshita & Sensō-ji flip by late morning

Both are pleasant early and a crush by 11:00 on a holiday. Do Sensō-ji at opening (Tue) and Takeshita on the Sunday morning; don't save either for a holiday afternoon.

It will be hot and humid

Late September is the tail of summer — Tokyo around 27–29°C. Rooftops and alleys are evening activities; carry water and use the depachika basements and museums to duck the afternoon heat.

Typhoon season is still open

September is the statistical peak. A "planned suspension" can cost a day; keep the plan flexible and don't prepay non-refundable ticket dates you can't move. teamLab and Shibuya Sky both let you rebook a slot within limits — check at purchase.

Don't buy the JR Pass

It is the most common expensive mistake on this exact route, and the arithmetic isn't close. The nationwide pass rose from about ¥29,650 to ¥50,000 in October 2023 and has never made sense for a Tokyo–Kyoto–Osaka loop since.

A 7-day pass would technically cover both long shinkansen legs — and still loses by about ¥21,000. Started on 24 Sep it runs through 30 Sep, catching Tokyo→Kyoto, the JR Nara-line hops on the 28th, and Shin-Osaka→Tokyo. It doesn't even cover the Kintetsu run from Nara into Osaka — that's not a JR line — and it excludes the Nozomi, so you'd be on slower Hikari trains. It's dead weight on Tokyo's subways and the Tobu line to Nikko too, the networks you actually use most. Buy individual tickets; tap an IC card for everything local.

What you should buy for the mountain leg: the Tobu Nikko All Area Pass (¥8,000 / 4 days) reaches Chuzenji and Yumoto; the cheaper ¥3,000 City Area pass does not go up the mountain, which is where you're spending the day.

The tattoos and the diet decide this leg. A traditional ryokan serves a fixed, fish-heavy kaiseki (no chicken option) and its onsen is communal (tattoos refused). Things that will otherwise catch you out

Suica top-ups reject Visa

Foreign-issued Visa cards cannot top up a digital Suica in Apple Wallet — a network-level block, not a bug. Use Mastercard or Amex, or load a physical card with cash. Tapping a Visa to pay a shop works fine.

Oversized bags need a free reservation

On the Tokaido shinkansen, luggage over 160 cm total (height + width + depth) requires a free reserved slot. Turn up without one and it's ¥1,000. Most check-in cases are under. Forwarding your bags sidesteps this entirely.

Typhoon season is still open

September is the statistical peak. Trains get "planned suspensions" announced a day ahead, and JR refunds them in full. It usually costs you one day, not the trip. Avoid non-refundable rates for the first half.

Tattoos and onsen

You're naked in an onsen, so clothing doesn't cover them, and many communal baths refuse tattooed guests outright. The reliable answer is a ryokan room with a private open-air bath — always permitted, and you bathe together. Both Euro hotels in Nikko have one in every room, which is exactly why they're the pick for your mountain leg.

Museums close Mondays — except this one

Mon 21 Sep is a public holiday, so museums open and shift their closure to Tue 22 Sep instead. Mon 28 Sep is your Nara + Uji travelling day — no matter, as Tōdai-ji, Byōdō-in and the shrines don't take a Monday closure the way city museums do.

It will be hot

Late September is the tail of summer, not autumn: Tokyo and Osaka around 27–29°C and humid. It breaks in the first days of October. Nikko at 1,400m is the exception — single-digit nights.
