## RYOKAN MECHANICS — the trap

- **Ryokan quote PER PERSON, including dinner + breakfast** ("一泊二食 / ippaku-nishoku").
  A "¥40,000" ryokan is **¥80,000 for two**. Look for "per guest / 一名様".
- Exceptions that price **per room**: Chuzenji Kanaya, KAI Nikko, and urban/modern ryokan (Celestine, Kanra).
- **Rhythm:** check-in from 15:00, **arrive by 17:00–18:00**; kaiseki is a fixed seating ~18:00–19:00, plated fresh.
  Check-out 10:00–11:00, breakfast 07:30–08:30. Treat as a locked 15:00→10:00 block.
  Do NOT book a late-arriving train on check-in day, or an ultra-early one the next morning.
- Arrange dietary substitutions in advance.
- **Reddit warning:** if you want to eat in town, don't take the half-board package — it chains you to a return time.

### Tattoos
Many onsen still refuse tattoos; ~half have loosened. **Most reliable workaround: a room with an in-room or
reservable private bath (kashikiri)** — always tattoo-OK, and you bathe together. Waterproof covers work only
for small pieces. Prioritize Ginyu / Kyuan / Ryuguden / Okunikko Yunomori (in-room baths).
Israeli sources name tattoo-tolerant options: Kurama Onsen near Kyoto, several Koyasan temple baths.

## TRANSPORT

### JR Pass — DO NOT BUY. Verdict is firm.

Nationwide pass: **7-day ¥50,000 / 14-day ¥80,000** (valid through 30 Sep 2026; rises to ¥53,000 / ¥84,000 on 1 Oct).
Rose from ~¥29,650 in Oct 2023.

Point-to-point for this itinerary, per person:
| Leg | Train | Fare |
|---|---|---|
| Tokyo → Kyoto | Hikari | ¥13,850 (Nozomi ¥14,170) |
| Kyoto ⇄ Osaka | JR Kyoto line, **no shinkansen** | ¥580 each way |
| Kyoto ⇄ Nara | JR Miyakoji rapid | ¥720 each way |
| Kyoto → Tokyo | Nozomi ¥14,720 / Hikari ¥14,400 | |
| **Total** | | **≈ ¥31,170** |

A 7-day pass started 25 Sep runs to 1 Oct and *would* cover both long legs — and still loses ~¥18,800.
It also **excludes the Nozomi**, and is dead weight on Tokyo subways, the Tobu line to Nikko,
and the Odakyu to Hakone. Nozomi supplement alone is ¥4,960 Tokyo–Kyoto.

### Regional passes worth buying
- **Hakone Free Pass** — **¥7,100 / 2 days**, ¥7,500 / 3 days from Shinjuku (incl. round-trip Odakyu).
  ¥6,000 / ¥6,400 if bought at Odawara. Covers Tozan railway, cablecar, ropeway, Lake Ashi boat, area buses,
  + 70 discounts. **Romancecar NOT included: +¥1,200 one way** (¥1,150 e-ticket).
- **Tobu Nikko All Area Pass** — **¥8,000 / 4 days**, reaches Chuzenji + Yumoto. japan-guide notes it
  "lost a lot of its value in 2025" (price doubled).
  **Nikko World Heritage / City Area Pass ¥3,000 / 2 days does NOT reach the lake** — where you're sleeping.
  Tobu Ltd Express surcharge ¥1,450–2,140 one way, not covered.
- **Kansai passes — skip.** Kansai Area Pass being discontinued (final use 1 Nov 2026). Kyoto⇄Nara round trip is only ¥1,440.

### Luggage
- **Oversized baggage on Tokaido shinkansen:** sum of 3 dimensions **>160 cm** needs a *free* reserved
  "oversized baggage area" seat. Turn up without one: **¥1,000**. Max 250 cm / 30 kg. Most check-in cases are under 160.
- **Takkyubin (Yamato) forwarding:** ~**¥2,000–3,200 per case** Tokyo↔Kyoto, budget ¥2,500. **Next-day standard**,
  allow 1–2 days. Hotel front desk does it; sender = current hotel, recipient = next hotel + arrival date.
  Konbini also accept drop-offs. **Send bags Tokyo→Kyoto the day before, travel with an overnight bag** —
  sidesteps the oversized reservation entirely. This came from the FB group and is the best logistics move of the trip.

### Money & connectivity
- **Suica/Pasmo:** anonymous cards back on sale since 1 Mar 2025 (¥500 deposit). **Welcome Suica Mobile**
  (iOS app, Mar 2025) — English UI, no Japanese Apple ID, foreign cards OK, valid 180 days. Best pick.
- ⚠ **Foreign-issued Visa cards CANNOT top up a digital Suica** in Apple Wallet — network-level block.
  Use Mastercard or Amex, or load a physical card with cash. Tapping a Visa to *pay a merchant* works fine.
- Android generally **cannot** add Suica without a Japan-region account / FeliCa hardware.
- eSIM: pick a **Docomo or au** network provider for rural reliability. Ubigi (~$14/10 GB/7 days) or Airalo.
- ~58% cashless (2025 METI). Cash still needed for small restaurants (ticket machines), temples, rural areas,
  small ryokan, coin lockers. Carry ¥15,000–30,000 pp; refill at **7-Eleven (Seven Bank)** or Japan Post ATMs.

## WEATHER & SEASON

| Location | Late Sept H/L | Early Oct H/L |
|---|---|---|
| Tokyo | ~27 / 21 °C | ~22 / 15 |
| Osaka | ~29 / 21 | ~23 / 15 |
| Kyoto | ~29 / 21 (basin, humid) | ~23 / 15 |
| Hakone (~700 m) | ~26 / 19–20 | ~19; lows 10–16 |
| Nikko town (~540 m) | high ~23 | ~17–18 / low ~10 |
| **Oku-Nikko (~1,400 m)** | avg ~15, **single-digit nights** | single-digit nights |

- Late Sept is the **tail of summer** — hot and humid. It breaks in early October.
- **Typhoons: Aug–Sept is the statistical peak.** JR announces "planned suspensions" (*keikaku unkyu*) a day
  ahead and **refunds cancelled trains in full** (up to a year to claim; Smart EX refunds from the phone).
  Usually costs one day, not the trip. Avoid non-refundable rates in the first half.
  The day *after* a typhoon often brings the year's clearest skies — best Fuji odds.
- **NO AUTUMN COLOUR anywhere on this route.** japan-guide: colours "start descending from the higher elevations
  of Yumoto Onsen in **early October**", Lake Chuzenji + Irohazaka peak **mid-to-late October**, Kegon Falls
  **late October**, Tokyo and Kyoto **second half of November**. Expect green.
- **Mt Fuji visibility:** September ≈ **10 fully-visible days / month (~33%)**, the weakest end of the year.
  October ~39% and improving. Best Dec–Jan. **Do not plan around Fuji.**

- **Senjogahara marsh trail (Oku-Nikko)** — **2.5–3 hrs**, ~8 km, mostly flat boardwalk, "relatively easy."

- **Hakone Round Course** — Tozan rail 35 min → cablecar 10 → ropeway 30 (Owakudani) → pirate ship 30 →

- **Senso-ji** — main hall 06:00–17:00, grounds always open, free.
- **Nara** — Todai-ji ~45 min; **half-day loop ~4 hrs.** Kyoto→Nara: JR Miyakoji rapid 45 min ¥720
  (stops at **Inari**), or Kintetsu ltd exp 35 min ¥1,280 (closer to the park, not JR-Pass valid).

### The two highest-leverage tactics
1. **Fushimi Inari at ~06:45** (mobbed by 08:00) — and go there **before** Nara, not on the way back.
   Inari station is on the JR Nara line between Kyoto and Nara, so it's genuinely en route.
2. **Kiyomizu-dera at 06:00** — the Sannenzaka/Ninenzaka lanes are empty too.
   You can only "dawn" one sight per morning; plan the day around it.
   (Arashiyama bamboo: arrive by 07:30–08:00; 10:00–15:00 is mobbed.)

### Monday closures
Nezu Museum and most Ueno museums close Mondays. **Mon 21 Sep is a holiday → they open and close Tue 22 Sep instead.**
Mon 28 Sep is a normal Monday (a Kyoto day — no museums planned, fine).

## PACING — Reddit consensus

- **1–2 major temples per day, maximum**, broken up with neighborhoods, food, walks.
  "Temple fatigue" comes from temple-only days, not from temples. Fushimi Inari "doesn't count" — it's a hike.
- **Nara is a half-day, not a full day.** Near-unanimous. Crowds thin sharply away from the station.
- **6 Tokyo nights is not too many** — it's the city people most wish they'd given *more* time.
- **Ending in Tokyo is explicitly endorsed** — shopping on the final days, carry it onto the plane.
- **Osaka 2 nights vs day trip is genuinely contested** — Reddit splits ~evenly. Osaka's real wins are
  street food (kuidaore), night energy, friendliness/value. It loses on temples/gardens/onsen.
  Osaka Castle is a **concrete reconstruction** with 30+ min queues — park + exterior is enough.
- **Hakone vs Nikko:** Reddit + japan-guide + Israeli sources lean **Hakone** (season-independent, on the
  Tokaido corridor, better ryokan density). Nikko wins on hiking + weather-proof headline sights.
  **User chose Nikko.** A thread exists from someone doing literally "Hakone, 2 nights, last week of September" —
  answer was that Hakone's value doesn't depend on seeing Fuji.
- Underrated adds that fit a 2-night rule: **Kanazawa (2n)**, **Takayama (2n)**.
  Koyasan (1n) and Nakasendo/Magome–Tsumago (day walk) break the rule but are high reward.
  Anti-burnout trick people love: **collect goshuin** (temple stamp book).
